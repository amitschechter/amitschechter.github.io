Concept Erasure via Attention Redirection CVPR Findings 2026
Amit Schechter, Rinon Gal, Ofir Kedem, Gal Chechik, Daniel Cohen-Or
CVPR Findings, 2026
Large text-to-image diffusion models can reproduce copyrighted or sensitive visual concepts, and while prior erasure methods suppress targets by fine-tuning model weights, they suffer from concept re-emergence under adversarial prompting, inpainting, or minor model edits. We introduce ARCE, which trains a per-concept eraser key that competes for attention probability mass in the cross-attention layers, surgically redirecting image patch attention away from target tokens toward a neutral anchor value — leaving the base model frozen and unrelated concepts intact.
